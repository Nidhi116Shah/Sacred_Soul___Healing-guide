üå± What Is Ashwagandha?

Ashwagandha is a small shrub with yellow flowers and fleshy roots, native to India, the Middle East, and parts of Africa. In Ayurveda, it is classified as a powerful adaptogen ‚Äî a plant that helps the body manage physical and emotional stress.

The name "Ashwagandha" comes from Sanskrit, meaning "smell of a horse," which refers to both its strong earthy scent and its traditional belief in imparting the strength and vitality of a horse.
